Executive Working Sessions: AI-Augmented Organization

Strategic paralysis is common when teams are flooded with AI noise. This session gives executive leaders the structure they need to frame risk, align around priorities, and turn AI strategy into a workable operating vision.

Book Darren Open Media Kit

What this workshop is for

This session is designed for leadership teams that need to decide where AI fits, how to govern it, and what to fund first. It is built for groups that want a practical conversation, clear decisions, and an artifact they can use after the workshop ends.

  • Audience: Senior leaders and decision-makers, up to 20 participants
  • Format: Two half-day working sessions, onsite or virtual
  • Duration: 2 half-days plus executive reinforcement sessions

What participants get

  • Unified AI strategy and governance alignment
  • Prioritized pilot initiatives and decision points
  • Executive decision memo and roadmap
  • Governance charter and roles matrix

Typical agenda

A workshop should leave the room with a plan, not just notes.

1. Frame the problem: what is changing, what matters, and what is at risk.

2. Align the leadership team on priorities, guardrails, and decision rights.

3. Convert the discussion into a concise roadmap and next-step plan.

4. Review the reinforcement actions needed after the session.

When this workshop is the right fit

Use it when the organization knows the problem, needs alignment quickly, and wants a practical path to execution.

Related next step

After the workshop, some teams move directly into advisory or follow-on implementation.
