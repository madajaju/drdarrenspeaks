Individual Capability Sessions: Becoming AI-Augmented

Foundational AI literacy is the base of reliable execution. This session develops individual workflow discipline, responsible use habits, and practical confidence for people who are expected to use AI well.

Book Darren Open Media Kit

What this workshop is for

This workshop is ideal when the organization needs to raise baseline capability across a broad group of staff. It helps people understand where AI fits, how to use it responsibly, and how to keep human judgment in the loop.

  • Audience: Individual contributors and staff levels, up to 40 participants
  • Format: Two half-day literacy and skill sessions
  • Duration: 2 half-days per cohort

What participants get

  • Improved AI literacy and confidence
  • Responsible use habits and judgment checks
  • Practical prompt and workflow discipline
  • A shared vocabulary for safe adoption

Typical agenda

People should leave with habits they can use right away.

1. Establish the fundamentals: what AI can and cannot do reliably.

2. Practice safe usage patterns and judgment checkpoints.

3. Apply the ideas to real tasks participants already perform.

4. Define the personal habits that support responsible use at scale.

When this workshop is the right fit

Use it when the organization wants consistent literacy, clearer usage habits, and a common baseline before moving into more specialized work.

Related next step

If the organization needs a broader leadership conversation first, start with an executive session.

FAQ

Is this session technical?

It is practical first. The focus is everyday work, judgment, and responsible adoption.

Can this be run for a mixed audience?

Yes. It works well for groups that need a common baseline before more specialized workshops.
